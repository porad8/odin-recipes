Cuban Ropa Vieja Recipe

Description

This is one of Cuba's most popular and beloved dishes. It is so popular that it's designated as one of the country's national dishes. Ropa Vieja, adapted from the Spanish term "old clothes" is made from shredded beef and vegetables that when ready resemble a heap of colorful rags.

Served over white rice, with a side of black beans and fried sweet plantains will make this dish truly unforgettable. Try it today.

Ingridients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 pounds beef flank steak
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 can tomato sauce (8 ounce)
  • 1 can tomato paste (6 ounce)
  • 1 green bell pepper, seeded and sliced into strips
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chooped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar

Steps

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add flank steak and cook until browned, about 4 minutes per side.

  2. Transfer steak to a slow cooker, pour in beef broth and tomato sauce. Add tomato paste, bell pepper, onion, garlic, cumin, cilantro, olive oil and vinegar. Stir until well blended.

  3. Cover and cook on low until the steak is tender, around 8 to 10 hours.

  4. Shred the steak in the slow cooker with two forks and serve. Enjoy!
